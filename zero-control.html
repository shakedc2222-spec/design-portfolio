Zero Control

A cyberpunk twist on classic board games — designed with AI as a creative partner.

The Challenge

To take the familiar format of a "Monopoly-style" game and give it an innovative, refreshing twist in the world of Cyberpunk. Additionally, we were required to use Artificial Intelligence (AI) as an integral part of all planning and execution stages.

The Solution

We designed a strategy game that combines a physical board with a digital app that eliminates the tedious parts of manual calculations. AI accompanied us every step of the way — from creating the content world and characters to the graphic design of the cards and the board. The result is a game that feels experiential and fluid, where technology connects players rather than hindering them.

The Characters

The game focuses on five rebel characters and one Gamemaster named Zero, whose goal is to hunt down the rebels and oversee the game. During the game, players carry out missions and engage in battles against each other and against Zero. We chose to give each character their own backstory to help players connect with them on a deeper level. We created the characters ourselves and refined them using generative tools such as Midjourney and Firefly.

The Cards & The Board

We designed the cards with a simpler design language to create more space for content — over 200 content cards, plus a separate resource deck and a villain deck for the Gamemaster. The board was built using hexagonal shapes, inspired by Catan, so the game never repeats itself — in a dark, cyberpunk-appropriate color palette.

The Packaging

We wanted the packaging to capture attention and convey the game's atmosphere — creating a box that would make people want to pick it up off the shelf and take it home. We chose to feature Zero overlooking the city, communicating both the game's name and its mood in one striking visual.

The App

Aligned with the game's futuristic theme, the app serves as its core, seamlessly connecting the physical and digital realms. By automating financial transfers, calculations, and status tracking, it eliminates the manual tedium of classic board games. We designed role-specific interfaces: players see their personal finances and others' status, while the Gamemaster (Zero) uses a dedicated dashboard.
